Technical Indicators

Select an analytical tool to explore its mathematical logic, signal triggers, and strategic deployment boundaries.

Category: Momentum & Market Strength | Default Period: 14

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

Core Mechanism

The RSI is a bounded oscillator that tracks the speed and size of recent price changes. By comparing the days a stock closes up against the days it closes down over a 14-period window, it fits market momentum onto a scale from 0 to 100.

Base Formula: $RSI = 100 - \frac{100}{1 + RS}$
Where $RS = \frac{\text{Average Gain}}{\text{Average Loss}}$

Strategic Execution

In standard charting, values above 70 indicate overbought conditions where a rally might be getting tired. Values below 30 point to oversold conditions where a stock might bounce. In broader macro trends, crossing above the 50 baseline confirmed structural upward momentum.

Category: Trend Tracking | Default: 12, 26, 9

Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

Core Mechanism

The MACD charts the relationship between two moving averages of a stock price. It takes a fast moving average and subtracts a slower one to plot a fluctuating line that shows trend acceleration before it completely shows up in the stock price.

MACD Line = 12-Period EMA - 26-Period EMA
Signal Line = 9-Period EMA of the MACD Line

Strategic Execution

Traders watch for the MACD line to cross over the slower Signal line. Crossing above it signals bullish momentum, while crossing below suggests a turning point to the downside. The center histogram shows the widening or narrowing gap between the two lines.

Category: Trend Following | Default: 10, 3

Supertrend

Core Mechanism

Supertrend is an overlay indicator that draws a continuous line directly on the price chart to map the current trend direction. It combines a simple moving average with a volatility measure to make sure ordinary daily noise does not trigger a false signal.

Strategic Execution

When the line flips below the stock price and turns green, it indicates a buy signal. When it flips above the price and turns red, it indicates a sell signal. It works well as a trailing stop loss during long, continuous market moves.

Category: Volatility & Stops | Default Acceleration: 0.02

Parabolic Stop and Reverse (Parabolic SAR)

Core Mechanism

The Parabolic SAR places small dots on the chart either above or below the price bars. It is designed to find turning points in a trend and automatically accelerates its tracking speed the longer a trend keeps running in one direction.

Strategic Execution

When the dots switch from being above the candles to underneath them, it shows a trend reversal upward. Because the indicator constantly creeps closer to the price over time, it provides a strict exit plan for protecting gains.

Category: Pure Momentum | Default: 5, 34

Awesome Oscillator (AO)

Core Mechanism

The Awesome Oscillator looks at market momentum by comparing recent market speed against a broader historical lookback. Instead of using closing prices, it looks at the midpoint of each daily candle to see if buying pressure is genuinely accelerating or slowing down.

Strategic Execution

The simplest signal is watching the oscillator cross the zero line. Crossing from negative to positive shows building bullish momentum, while dropping below zero shows sellers are taking control.
